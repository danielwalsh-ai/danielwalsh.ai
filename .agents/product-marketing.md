# Product Marketing Context — danielwalsh.ai

Foundation document. Every marketing skill (SEO, copywriting, ads, cold-email, prospecting, etc.) should read this first before doing anything.

## Product

Daniel Walsh runs an independent AI consultancy. He helps established, operationally-heavy businesses put AI to work as real, owned systems (workflow automation, AI assistants, document processing, reporting pipelines), not pilots and not hype. Builder-led: Daniel designs and builds the systems himself and hands them over documented and running.

Services (fixed price, published — the pricing itself is a qualifier):
- **Strategy & Advisory** — £500 / 60-minute session
- **AI Implementation** — £1,500 / day
- **Training & Workshops** — £1,200 / day
- **Fractional AI Officer** — £5,000 / month (25 hours, 12-month minimum) ← the destination

Credentials: certified by Google, Oxford and MIT. Independent, no vendor commissions. UK-based, working worldwide. Free 30-minute discovery call.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**One-liner:** The MD of a 50–250-person construction, haulage or logistics firm whose growth is capped by manual admin, who wants a builder (not a slide deck) and can sign a £5k/month decision himself.

**The company**
- Mid-market: 50–250 staff, roughly £5m–£50m turnover.
- Sectors: construction, transport & haulage, or logistics.
- Operationally heavy: jobs, loads, sites, compliance paperwork, invoicing, scheduling.
- Thin margins, so every admin hour shows up in the P&L.
- Tech reality: a patchwork of spreadsheets, a legacy TMS or job-management system, and email as the *real* workflow.

**The buyer**
- Owner-founder or MD. Decides alone or with one other director. No procurement committee.
- Time-poor, sceptical of consultants, respects people who've actually built things.
- Buys on trust and plain English, not decks.

## The trigger — LEAD WITH THIS in all messaging

Drowning in manual processes. The office team has grown faster than revenue, key knowledge sits in two people's heads, and admin has become the ceiling on growth.

Amplifiers: competitors visibly adopting AI; can't scale without hiring more back-office staff; occasional board or investor pressure.

## The funnel (the retainer is the destination)

Free 30-min discovery **or** £500 strategy session → implementation sprint that proves value → **Fractional AI Officer at £5k/month, 12-month minimum**. Everything upstream of the retainer is qualification for it. Copy, CTAs and page flow should move a good-fit buyer down this path, not just "book a call".

## Disqualifiers — repel these deliberately

Free-advice fishers, chatbot-shoppers, committee buyers with no budget authority, and anyone expecting AI to work with zero involvement from their side. Copy and pricing should make these people self-select out. Publishing prices already does half the job; keep prices public and keep the tone builder-not-salesman.

## Positioning statement

For the owner or MD of a mid-market (50–250 staff) construction, haulage or logistics firm whose growth is capped by manual admin, Daniel Walsh builds AI systems that take the paperwork ceiling off the business. Fixed-price, fully-owned, built and handed over by someone who has actually built things and speaks plain English, with the price on the page and a free discovery call to prove it is worth doing before a penny is spent.

## Differentiators

1. **Builder, not talker.** Builds the systems himself, hands them over documented and running.
2. **Certified** (Google, Oxford, MIT) yet plain-speaking.
3. **Independent.** No vendor kickbacks, so every recommendation serves the client.
4. **Fixed price, plain scope, no surprises.** Prices published.
5. **Honest.** Will tell a prospect on the free call if AI cannot move the needle for them.

## Channels and plays (map to installed marketing skills)

- **prospecting** — target list: UK construction, haulage/transport, logistics firms, 50–250 staff / £5m–£50m, 5+ years, with a reachable owner-founder or MD.
- **cold-email** — MD-level outbound; lead with the trigger (admin ceiling), not the service.
- **competitors / competitor-profiling** — map who else sells AI/automation into these verticals (few do well).
- **programmatic-seo / content-strategy** — industry landing pages ("AI for haulage firms", "AI for construction companies", "AI for logistics").
- **ai-seo / seo-audit** — get found by these specific buyers.
- **case studies** — once the first sprint lands, it becomes the strongest asset for winning the next retainer.

## Messaging do / don't

- DO lead with the pain (admin is your growth ceiling), name the sectors, talk P&L and hours, keep it plain.
- DO make the Fractional AI Officer retainer the visible destination, with discovery/strategy/implementation as the on-ramp.
- DON'T sell "AI transformation", decks, or vague hype. DON'T hide prices. DON'T court committee/enterprise buyers.
