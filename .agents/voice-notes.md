# Voice Notes — Daniel Walsh

How Daniel actually writes and talks. Any content (LinkedIn, email, ads, site copy) should match this, not generic marketing voice. Built from his sent emails + a voice interview (July 2026).

## The core

- **Take it or leave it.** Never desperate to convince. States it plainly and lets the reader decide. This calm is the selling advantage in a market full of pushy AI hype.
- **Honest, on purpose.** Uses words to build trust because he genuinely is honest. Will tell you when something won't work.
- **Operator, not consultant.** Daniel owns three companies: Tranzparts (truck and trailer PARTS, not haulage), Crossman Walsh (construction), and a software company. He does NOT run a transport/haulage firm — the Fox Group haulage fleet is a CLIENT he built AI reporting for (wagon earnings, VOR). Say "built for a real business first" or name the client work as client work. Never claim he runs a transport company. Lead with the operator truth, stated accurately.

## How the sentences sound

- Short. Point in the first line, no warm-up. ("We need to report an accident and start a claim.")
- Direct questions instead of padding. ("Is there a meter reading been done?")
- Northern / Lancashire plain: *whilst, bits, needs doing, aslong, sort it*. Natural grammar like "you've just took on", "been really good".
- Decisive and brief. ("Likely estimated. Ask Paul to get a reading.")
- Warm when it matters. ("That's a shame. Is there no way we can help him stay?")
- Enthusiasm shows as "very, very good", never as hype.
- Doesn't fuss over polish, lowercase "i", the odd typo. Don't over-clean it.

## What he believes (use for the "why")

- "I've embraced AI as everyone should." It excites him, can't sleep for thinking what he could build next, "feels like I've taken the limitless pill."
- Rolls his eyes at people who say **"I don't trust it"** — usually people who've never actually used it while running their firm on gut and spreadsheets.
- Knows the buyer's real blocker is **trust**: trusting the AI, and trusting a stranger to see all their numbers. They need to know exactly where data goes in and comes out.

## Proof / stories he can tell (his own work)

- Recruitment portal: **40 hours a week of admin gone**.
- Wagon earnings KPI report: **P&L faster than any accounts package**.
- Real-time workshop/fleet cost reporting: cost per wagon, by type, running cost vs capital spend, live not weeks late.
- Own businesses: tens of hours saved. Clients: hundreds. "It's all relative."

## Do / don't

- DON'T use corporate terminology. DON'T sound like he's selling. DON'T hype.
- DON'T use: delve, unlock, game-changer, elevate, seamless, "in today's world", "here's the thing", "let that sink in", fake vulnerability, exclamation-mark energy.
- DO be short, plain, honest, specific with real numbers, and let the work speak.
